<SUMMARY>
Qatar announced a ceasefire deal between Israel and Hamas.
-  Indirect talks between the two sides have been ongoing since a previous ceasefire ended in December 2023.
-  Hamas launched a major attack on Israel in October, resulting in the deaths of approximately 1,200 Israelis and taking over 250 hostages.
-  Israel launched a counter-offensive in response, leading to the reported deaths of at least 46,000 Palestinians according to Hamas.
-  This new ceasefire agreement is a significant development in the ongoing conflict.


</SUMMARY>
<HIGHLIGHTS>
- A ceasefire deal between Israel and Hamas has been announced by Qatar Prime Minister Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim bin Jabr Al Thani .
- Israel and Hamas have held on-off indirect talks for a ceasefire and hostage-prisoner exchange since the first ceasefire ended on 1 December 2023.
- Hamas launched an unprecedented attack on southern Israel on 7 October 2023, killing about 1,200 people and taking 251 back to Gaza as hostages.
- The attack triggered a massive Israeli offensive on Gaza, during which at least 46,000 Palestinians have been killed, according to the Hamas-run health ministry.

A ceasefire deal between Israel and Hamas has been announced by Qatar Prime Minister Mohammed bin Abdulrahman bin Jassim bin Jabr Al Thani .

Israel and Hamas have held on-off indirect talks for a ceasefire and hostage-prisoner exchange since the first ceasefire ended on 1 December 2023.

Hamas launched an unprecedented attack on southern Israel on 7 October 2023, killing about 1,200 people and taking 251 back to Gaza as hostages.

The attack triggered a massive Israeli offensive on Gaza, during which at least 46,000 Palestinians have been killed, according to the Hamas-run health ministry.
